Amazon is relaunching its FBA New Selection Program on July 30 with substantially larger incentives for launching new branded products — and a deadline that could quietly cost inattentive sellers their benefits.

## What's changing on July 30

The New Selection Program (2026) replaces the existing program for new branded FBA ASINs and stacks several launch subsidies together:

- **Referral fees capped at 10%** (or your existing rate, whichever is lower) on the first 100 units of each qualifying new ASIN, and **5% on the next 100 units**, delivered as instant fee credits.
- **$50 in coupon fee credits and $75 in Vine enrollment credits** (applied at the middle tier), usable within the first 60 days.
- **Free storage, free customer returns, and free liquidations on the first 200 units for the first 120 days**, plus exemption from the low-inventory-level fee and storage utilization surcharge on those units.

Eligibility requires a professional selling plan in good standing and, where assigned, a trailing six-month Inventory Performance Index score of 300 or higher.

## The deadline most sellers will miss

Sellers already enrolled in the current New Selection Program are migrated automatically — but only as an introductory offer covering new branded ASINs launched between July 30 and October 31, 2026. **To keep receiving benefits on ASINs listed after October 31, sellers must actively confirm enrollment under the updated program terms before that date.**

Benefits already earned during the introductory window are not clawed back. But a seller who takes no action will find that products launched from November onward get nothing — right as Q4 launch season begins.

One more wrinkle worth knowing: where an ASIN qualifies for both this program and Amazon's New Seller Incentives, overlapping credit types draw down the New Seller Incentives first, with the 2026 program's versions applying only after those are exhausted.

## What this means for your brand

For multichannel brands, the math on Amazon product launches just shifted. A capped 10% referral fee on early units meaningfully lowers the cost of validating new SKUs on the marketplace, and the 120-day waiver window covers the riskiest stretch of a launch — the period where slow sellers normally bleed storage fees.

Three practical moves:

1. **Time new-SKU launches for after July 30** where you can. Launching a new branded ASIN this week instead of next week forfeits the expanded credits.
2. **Put October 31 on the calendar now.** If your team sees "automatically enrolled" on the seller incentive dashboard, treat it as provisional until enrollment is confirmed under the new terms.
3. **Use the subsidy as a testing budget.** The fee relief on the first 200 units is effectively a discount on demand validation — pair it with a disciplined read on conversion and reviews before committing to deeper inventory.
